# Generic MDPI Academic Article Quality Guideline

This guideline defines the minimum quality bar for a well-formed academic journal article.
It is used by the LLM judge to evaluate articles on structural and academic quality —
NOT on topical relevance to any specific research question.

## Required Structure (IMRAD)
- Abstract (50–300 words): objective, methods, key result, and conclusion
- Keywords section (5–10 terms)
- 1. Introduction: background, research gap, objectives
- 2. Materials and Methods (or Methodology): replicable, detailed
- 3. Results: objective findings, data, statistics
- 4. Discussion: interpretation, comparison with literature, limitations
- 5. Conclusions: summary of contributions, future work
- References section: ≥15 numbered citations in MDPI format

## Citation Requirements
- All major claims backed by inline citations [N]
- At least 15 references total
- References formatted as: Author, A.; Title. Journal Year, Vol, Pages. DOI
- No plagiarism — sources summarised, not copied verbatim

## Writing Quality
- Formal academic English (third-person or passive voice preferred)
- No speculative or unsubstantiated statements
- Sentences ≤35 words; paragraphs 4–7 sentences
- Technical terms defined on first use

## Visual Elements
- At least one of: table, figure, or equation
- All figures/tables captioned and cited in the text

## Verdict Rules
- PASS: all required sections present, ≥10 citations, abstract ≥50 words, formal language
- FAIL: any required section missing, <10 citations, abstract <50 words, or informal language
