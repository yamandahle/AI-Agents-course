# Writing Profile — Characters (Voice & Tone)

## Author Voice
**Formal academic** — the article represents the author's academic work submitted for a university course. The voice is authoritative, precise, and impersonal.

## Persona
- Name: Nagham Mansour
- Role: Graduate student in AI and Agents course
- Expertise: Software engineering and AI systems
- Writing style: Methodical, evidence-based, clear

## Tone Rules
| Rule | Example — Wrong | Example — Correct |
|---|---|---|
| No first person | "I found that..." | "The results indicate that..." |
| No exclamation marks | "This is incredible!" | "This result is significant." |
| No colloquialisms | "This is pretty cool." | "This demonstrates notable performance." |
| Avoid passive overuse | "It was shown by..." | "Smith et al. showed..." |
| Cite before concluding | "AI is clearly superior." | "AI outperformed baseline by 15% [1]." |

## Sentence Structure
- Maximum sentence length: **25 words**
- Preferred sentence length: 15–20 words
- Avoid nested clauses more than 2 levels deep
- Start sentences with the subject where possible

## Paragraph Structure
- Length: **4–7 sentences** per paragraph
- First sentence: topic sentence (states the paragraph's claim)
- Middle sentences: evidence, data, citations
- Last sentence: synthesis or transition to next paragraph
- One idea per paragraph — no paragraph covers two unrelated points

## Active vs Passive Voice
- **Active preferred** for methodology: "The algorithm processes input data..."
- **Passive acceptable** for results: "The model was trained on..."
- **Never passive** for the author's own contribution: "I designed..." → "This study designed..."

## Prohibited Phrases
- "As we can see..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Obviously" / "Clearly" (unless supported by data)
- "Very important" / "Extremely significant" (use the actual metric)
- "In today's world..." (too generic — be specific about the year/context)
- "In conclusion, we have seen..." (for the last paragraph of body sections)
